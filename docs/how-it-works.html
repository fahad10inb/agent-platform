How one message flows

Message → reply, in about 3 seconds.

1

A customer texts the number on WhatsApp.

2

Meta instantly pings your app (a "webhook" — Meta knocking on your server's door).

3

Your app asks the AI for a reply — handing it that business's memory, listings & rules.

4

The AI writes a reply and does the work — captures the lead, books the slot.

5

The reply goes back on WhatsApp, and the owner gets an email alert.

The follow-up loop

It doesn't stop at
the first reply.

A lead rarely books on message one. So the system keeps every lead warm three ways — automatically, and never lets one go cold.

The one catch: the number

Every WhatsApp number is one of two kinds.

🧪 Test number (you have this)

Works right now — the AI really replies. But only 5 phone numbers you pre-approve can message it.

Great for demos · useless for real customers

✅ Real number

Anyone can message it — a real business line. The catch: it first needs Meta Business Verification.

Appendix

WhatsApp,
click by click.

The ~15-minute setup for a real number — for the day a client says yes. Two sides: collect the keys from Meta, then plug them into your app.

The shape of it

Everything is just moving 5 things
from Meta into your app.

① On Meta you collect

  • Phone number ID
  • WhatsApp Account (WABA) ID
  • A permanent access token
  • The app secret
  • A verify token (you invent this)

② On your side you

  • Paste those onto Render
  • Set the client's number ID in the dashboard
  • Subscribe their account to your app
  • Send a test message
Meta side · ① get the number

Add the real number & grab its two IDs.

1

Have a Meta Business account (business.facebook.com).

2

Your app → add the WhatsApp product → open API Setup.

3

Add phone number → verify it by SMS or call. (a number NOT already on the WhatsApp app)

4

Copy the Phone number ID and the WhatsApp Account (WABA) ID.

Meta side · ② the permanent key

The token from API Setup dies in 24h.
Make one that never expires.

1

Business Settings → Users → System Users → create reception-bot.

2

Add Assets → add the App AND the WABA → Full control.

3

Generate token → scopes whatsapp_business_messaging + whatsapp_business_management → copy it.

⚠️ The #1 thing that breaks it: forgetting to add the WABA as an asset (step 2). Without it the token looks fine but can't send — "access denied".
Meta side · ③ webhook & secret

Tell Meta where to knock.

1

App → WhatsApp → Configuration → Callback URL:

Callback URL  https://agent-platform-mivq.onrender.com/whatsapp/webhook
2

Verify token = any secret string you pick (must match your server's).

3

Subscribe to the messages field.

4

App → Settings → Basic → copy the App Secret.

Your side · plug it in

Paste the 5 things where they go.

WHATSAPP_ACCESS_TOKEN  = the permanent token
WHATSAPP_VERIFY_TOKEN  = your verify string
WHATSAPP_APP_SECRET  = the app secret
– remove WHATSAPP_SKIP_SIGNATURE

Those three go in Render → Environment. Then in your dashboard, open the client's business and set whatsapp_phone_id = the Phone number ID.

The step everyone forgets

Subscribe their account
to your app.

Run your /admin/whatsapp-subscribe for their WABA id. Without it, inbound messages never reach you.

🕳️ Meta's "Try it out" / API-Setup flow silently re-points webhooks to Meta's own test app and unsubscribes yours — messages just stop, with zero errors. If inbound ever goes quiet, re-run subscribe. (Use /admin/whatsapp-status to check.)

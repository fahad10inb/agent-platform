# What people actually pay + is this market dying? — researched 2026-07-13

Multi-source research, every claim adversarially fact-checked (a claim needed 2-of-3 skeptics to
REFUTE it to be killed). 13 findings survived. Sources cited inline.

---

## VERDICT (read this if nothing else)

1. **The market is NOT declining.** Every claim asserting decline/consolidation was **REFUTED**
   (0-3 votes). No verified vendor shutdowns, no down-rounds, no price compression found.
2. **The only hard directional signal points UP:** Structurely (real-estate AI lead-qualification)
   **RAISED prices ~25% in April 2026**. Willingness-to-pay in this exact niche is *rising*.
3. **Our AED 749 price is TOO LOW. AED 1,499 is about right.** (The churn-cliff data below.)
4. **The durable money is NOT in small agencies.** Solo–10 agents is the *worst* segment.
5. **The portals are NOT in our lane** — they're doing consumer search + lead scoring. None of
   them answers a brokerage's own inbound calls/WhatsApp. That lane is still open.

## 1. What each segment actually pays

| Vendor / segment | Price | Model | Real annual outlay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Generic SMB AI receptionist** (Rosie) | $49 / $149 / $299 per month | **Flat per company + minute buckets** (NOT per-seat); ~$0.15–0.20 per included minute | ~$490–$3,600/yr. But *booking + warm transfer only start at the $149 tier* → a brokerage that wants booking is a **~$1,788/yr** customer |
| **Real-estate AI-SDR** (Structurely) | **$499/mo** (Team) · **$999/mo** (Company) | **Annual contract** + **$2,000–2,500 one-time setup** + usage credits ($0.06–0.08) | **~$8,000 (Team) – $14,500 (Company) first year** |
| **Portal, per-seat** (Zillow Pro) | **$138 / agent / month** (~$1,656/yr/agent) | Per-seat; bundles Follow Up Boss CRM | Often **$0** — free to Zillow Preferred partners through 2026 |
| **Support AI, per-outcome** (Intercom Fin) | **$0.99 per resolved ticket** (+$49/mo base incl. 50) | Per-outcome — *a support model, not a real-estate model* | varies |

**Which pricing model is winning: HYBRID.** Bessemer's AI Pricing Playbook (Feb 2026, census of
200+ AI vendors): hybrid (base subscription + usage/outcome tier) rose **27% → 41%** adoption in
12 months, while **pure per-seat fell 21% → 15%**. Bessemer explicitly recommends hybrid as the
default when demand/value is uncertain. → *A pure flat fee is slightly off-model; flat base +
included minutes + overage is the shape to use.*

Sources: heyrosie.com/pricing · structurely.com/pricing · nurtureos.io ISA cost survey 2026 ·
inman.com (Zillow Pro nationwide, 2026-07-09) · fin.ai pricing · bvp.com/atlas AI pricing playbook

## 2. THE most important number we found — the churn cliff

**ChartMogul first-party billing data, ~3,500 software companies (2025):** retention of AI products
scales sharply with price point.

| Price band | Gross revenue retention | Net revenue retention |
|---|---|---|
| **Under $50/mo** | **23% GRR** | 32% NRR |
| **$50 – $249/mo** | **45% GRR** | 61% NRR |
| **Above $250/mo** | **70% GRR** | **85% NRR** |

**AED 749/mo ≈ $204/mo → lands in the 45%-GRR band. AED 1,499/mo ≈ $408/mo → clears the $250
threshold into the 70%-GRR band.**

Cheap AI subscriptions are the ones that churn out. Our proposed band straddles the cliff exactly.

Source: chartmogul.com/reports/saas-retention-the-ai-churn-wave · growthunhinged.com

## 3. The buy-vs-build (DIY) question

**The cost floor is cheap and public.** Retell AI: **$0.07–$0.31/min all-in**, $0 upfront, no
subscription — itemized as $0.055/min voice infra + $0.015–0.040/min TTS + $0.003–0.16/min LLM.
Realistic production configs land **$0.13–$0.31/min**.

→ **A brokerage running ~500 min/month incurs ~$65–155/mo (AED 240–570) of raw infrastructure.**

**What that means for us:** at **AED 749** we'd keep only ~**AED 180–500/mo gross margin** on a
busy tenant — thin. At **AED 1,499** the margin is comfortable. *This is a second, independent
argument for the higher price.*

**But is DIY actually eating vendors?** **No verified evidence.** Every claim asserting a DIY/
consolidation wave was refuted under adversarial checking. The cheap floor exists; it is not
visibly killing paid vendors.

Source: retellai.com/pricing · cloudtalk.io · cekura.ai

## 4. Are the portals eating this?

**They're in the water — but not in our lane.**

- **Bayut (UAE):** BayutGPT — natural-language property search over rental yields, community data,
  DLD transactions. Shipped a property-search app **inside ChatGPT** (June 2026, a UAE first).
  **Critically: BayutGPT launched in SEPTEMBER 2023 — it has coexisted with third-party UAE
  proptech vendors for nearly three years without visibly killing them.**
- **Rightmove (UK):** AI **vendor-scoring engine** (predicts seller likelihood) powering two agent
  products, plus an **AI reply writer**. Valuation leads to agents **up 50% YoY** (Jan 2026),
  attributed partly to AI.
- **Zillow (US):** Zillow Pro at $138/seat with readiness signals + Follow Up Boss.

**The pattern that matters:** every verified portal product occupies the **consumer-search** slice
or the **lead-scoring** slice. **None of them answers a brokerage's own inbound calls or WhatsApp.**
That is the lane a small vendor can still own.

**The warning:** portals DO bundle away *single-feature* products. BayutGPT generates listing
descriptions for free — absorbing exactly what standalone AI listing-copy vendors charge for.
**→ Any single-feature AI product is a bad bet in the UAE. Depth (a full operator) is the defense.**

Sources: bayut.com/mybayut/bayutgpt-ai · gulfnews.com · entrepreneur.com/en-ae ·
propertyindustryeye.com · estateagenttoday.co.uk · inman.com · zillow.mediaroom.com

## 5. Where the durable money is (ranked)

1. **10–50 agent brokerages and property DEVELOPERS** ← **the target.** Real inbound volume, a
   budget owner who can sign, and tolerance for annual contracts *and* setup fees.
2. **50+ brokerages** — bigger contract, but procurement gatekeepers a solo founder will struggle
   to get past. Go here once you have logos.
3. **Solo–10 agents** — **LOWEST value.** This is precisely the sub-$250, self-serve, **23–45% GRR**
   cohort most exposed to DIY tooling *and* to $49 generic receptionists. Use them for logos and
   learning, not for revenue.

## What we should change

- **Price at AED 1,499/mo, not 749.** It clears the retention cliff, keeps healthy margin over the
  ~AED 240–570 infra cost, and *still undercuts Structurely's $499/mo entry by ~20%.*
- **Add a setup / onboarding fee of AED 3,000–7,000.** Structurely charges $2,000–2,500 setup and
  customers pay it. It front-loads cash and raises perceived value.
- **Restructure as hybrid:** flat base + included minutes/conversations + overage (per Bessemer).
- **Retarget: aim at 10–50 agent brokerages and developers**, not solo agents.
- **Stay deep, never single-feature.** The portals bundle away point solutions; they do not build
  the agency's own inbound operator.

## Honest gaps in this research
- **UAE-specific vendor pricing is thin** — the price points above are US/UK benchmarks. Validate
  against what a real Dubai brokerage says they'd pay (that's what the first 5 sales calls are for).
- The "market is declining" question came back **unresolved-but-leaning-no**: we found no evidence
  *for* decline, which is not the same as proof of growth.
